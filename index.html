        How Lag Not! works

        Lag Not! generates a personalized protocol based on peer-reviewed circadian biology research. It accounts for your direction of travel, timezone difference, and flight timing to give you science-backed recommendations across six key levers:

        ☀️ Light exposure

        Light is the most powerful zeitgeber (time-giver) for your circadian clock. The phase response curve for light means that light at the right time advances or delays your clock by up to 2 hours per day. For eastward travel, morning light at the destination helps; for westward, evening light. The app calculates sunrise/sunset at your destination to give you precise windows.

        💊 Melatonin

        Melatonin at 0.5 mg (physiological dose) taken at the right time shifts your clock. Doses of 5–10 mg (common OTC) are no more effective for clock-shifting and impair sleep quality. For eastward travel, 0.5 mg at destination bedtime for the first few nights is recommended.

        🍽️ Fasting protocol

        A 12–16 hour fast ending at 7 AM destination time resets your peripheral clocks (liver, gut) independently of your brain clock — discovered at Harvard Medical School. Breaking the fast with a protein-rich breakfast at destination local morning is particularly effective.

        🌙 Sleep shifting

        For trips crossing 3+ time zones, shifting bedtime 30–60 minutes per night in the days before departure pre-adapts your clock. The app linearly interpolates your sleep window across the recovery days.

        🏃 Exercise

        Morning exercise at the destination reinforces phase advance (eastward); afternoon exercise is fine for westward. Exercise affects core body temperature, which is tightly coupled to your circadian rhythm.

        ☕ Caffeine

        Caffeine's half-life is 5–6 hours. The app sets a cutoff 10 hours before your target bedtime to protect sleep quality. On arrival days, caffeine is recommended to push through to local bedtime.
